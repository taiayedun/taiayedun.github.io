Research Interview

Maya

Maya is a African American 23 years old and graduated from the University of Illinois at Chicago in 2022. She is an aspiring content creator and fashion doll maker. Maya decided to get locs about a month ago.

“Yes I am aware of the negative part of people considering locks to be dirty or unprofessional and I definitely had that like ideology growing up”

Kim

An African American natural hairstylist and loctician. She has 6 years of experience being a Natural hairstylist and 2 years of experience being a Cosmetologist.

“My advice for people who are interested in starting or just started their loc journey would be to have patience.”

Kehinde (soundboard)

Kehinde is a Nigerian American 23 years old and graduated from Illinois State University in 2023. She is an aspiring Fashion Stylist striving to work in Production and Film. This was one of my favorite interviews because it felt more like a conversation and we were bouncing ideas, opinions, and comments off of one another.

“Within 2013-2015 I feel like that's when locs more [specifically] dreads had a negative connotation, but I feel like now since we see more celebrities wearing locs and embracing their natural hair nowadays locs doesn’t have a negative connotation when it comes to locs on women, I don't know about men. Society associates’ men who have locs as criminals.”

Want some advice?

How long should you wait to retie?

It's more than just watching sticking to a specific timeline; it's about knowing when to give your hair that TLC it needs while still letting it do its natural thing. So you decide when or talk to your loctician about any concerns you may have.

Does your hair feel or look dry?

Hey fam, feeling like your starter locs or dreads are drier than the Sahara? Don't stress! I got you covered with some essential tips: In a spray bottle, add water with a little bit of oil.
